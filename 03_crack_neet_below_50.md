## H2: Why Students Score Below 50% in NEET Preparation

Before fixing the problem, understand why it happens:

### H3: Weak Conceptual Foundation
NEET questions are concept-based, not memory-based. Students who memorised facts without understanding the underlying concept struggle when questions are framed differently than expected.

### H3: No Structured Study Plan
Studying randomly — jumping between subjects, covering easy topics repeatedly, avoiding difficult ones — leads to massive gaps in preparation.

### H3: Not Enough Practice Testing
Many students study for months without taking a single timed mock test. When they finally sit in exam conditions, they run out of time and panic. Practice without testing is incomplete preparation.

### H3: Ignoring Weak Subjects
Most students have one subject — often Physics — that they avoid because it feels too hard. Avoiding it does not make it go away. It compounds the problem every week.

## H2: A Realistic 6-Month Plan to Improve Your NEET Score

### H3: Month 1-2 — Rebuild the Foundation

Do not attempt full mock tests yet. Focus on:

- Going back to NCERT textbooks for all three subjects
- Understanding concepts, not memorising them
- Making short notes in your own words for each chapter
- Completing NCERT exercise questions for every chapter covered

This feels slow. It is not. A strong foundation in Month 1-2 pays off massively in Months 4-6.

### H3: Month 3-4 — Subject-Wise Practice

Once the foundation is in place:

- Solve previous year NEET questions chapter by chapter
- Focus extra time on your weakest subject
- Take subject-wise timed tests (Physics alone, Chemistry alone, Biology alone)
- Review every wrong answer — understand why it was wrong, not just what the right answer was

### H3: Month 5-6 — Full Mock Tests and Revision

- Take one full NEET mock test every week under exact exam conditions (180 minutes, no breaks, no phone)
- Analyse your score after every test — which subjects, which chapters, which question types are pulling you down?
- Spend revision time on weak areas, not comfortable ones
- Do not start new topics in Month 6 — only revise and test

## H2: Common Mistakes Low-Scoring Students Make

Avoid these:

- **Studying more hours without changing the method** — if something is not working, studying it for longer does not fix it
- **Skipping mock tests because they feel discouraging** — low mock test scores are information, not failure. Use them.
- **Focusing only on Biology** — Biology has the most questions, but Physics and Chemistry can make or break your total score
- **Changing study material constantly** — pick one reliable source per subject and master it completely
- **Comparing your progress to toppers** — measure yourself against your own previous score, not someone else's current score
